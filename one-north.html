    A 200-hectare development strategically positioned in the heart of Singapore, one-north is designed to host a cluster of world-class research facilities and business park space, all built to support the growth of Biomedical Sciences, Infocomm Technology (ICT), Media, Physical Sciences and Engineering. Combined with educational institutes, residences and recreational amenities, it creates an ideal work-live-play-learn environment conducive for creative minds to excel and innovation to flourish. This makes it a great melting pot of talent, ideas, and business opportunities.

    Testbedding footprint: one-north

    18 Bigbelly Smart Bins are deployed across 6 locations in JTC’s premises including one-north. The Smart Bins are able to gauge waste levels and activate clearing of the bins only when they are full. This optimises the collection response time and reduces frequency for waste collection, thereby reducing the manpower required to clear the bins.

    Testbedding footprint: one-north

    JTC is the first government agency in Singapore to allow the testing of AVs on a public road network. The trial site in the one-north offers opportunities to test and optimise the technical capabilities and infrastructural requirements necessary for AVs to operate safely on public roads. Test-bedding AVs in complex urban environments with changing traffic volumes and a myriad of driving behaviour allow self-driving algorithms to be further developed. This is crucial in order for the AVs to be validated in more complex environments in other parts of Singapore.

    Testbedding footprint: one-north

    A first of its kind, the IEMS connects to various Building Management Systems (BMS) to extract and analyse building equipment data from systems like air conditioning and mechanical ventilation systems. The IEMS detects faults and provides operational recommendations to assist Facilities Management staff in resolving these issues. This also eliminates dependence on data from BMS vendors, and translates to energy savings, increased efficiency and improved user experience.

    Testbedding footprint: one-north

    This pilot programme saw the application of a proprietary remote sensor at Fusionopolis. With the sensor, smart devices can remotely control and manage building facilities through Wi-Fi, Bluetooth and cloud hosting. The programme was initiated to improve JTC’s customer service, increase energy savings and make remote connectivity more efficient while optimising the use of existing building resources.

    Testbedding footprint: one-north

    This Solution aims to provide an energy-efficient and cost-effective wireless solution for the management of lighting on streets, and industrial and commercial areas. Initial testing is being conducted at the Biopolis One B2 car park in one-north, using energy efficient LED lights and smart wireless occupancy sensors to enhance the building’s energy savings. The Solution will dim lights in the absence of pedestrians or moving vehicles. It is expected to save more than 50% of the car park's current energy consumption.

    This project seeks ideas for innovative and cost-efficient technologies that can convert excavated rocks from Jurong Rock Caverns into higher value road construction materials - such as B1 and W3B asphaltic wearing courses - that meet regulatory requirements. The project hopes that the materials converted from the excavated rocks can be a cost-effective and viable alternative to current granite aggregates used in road construction.
