    one-north

    A 200-hectare development strategically positioned in the heart of Singapore, one-north is designed to host a cluster of world-class research facilities and business park space, all built to support the growth of Biomedical Sciences, Infocomm Technology (ICT), Media, Physical Sciences and Engineering. Combined with educational institutes, residences and recreational amenities, it creates an ideal work-live-play-learn environment conducive for creative minds to excel and innovation to flourish. This makes it a great melting pot of talent, ideas, and business opportunities.

    18 Big Belly Smart Bins are deployed across 6 locations in JTC premises. BigBelly is able to gauge waste levels and activate clearing of the bin only when it is full. This optimises the collection response time and reduces frequency for waste collection, thereby reducing manpower requirements to clear the bins as opposed to traditional clearing of the bins even when it is not full.

    Being the first agency to allow the testing of AVs on a public road network, the one-north and CleanTech Park trial sites offers opportunities to test and optimise the technical capabilities and infrastructural requirements necessary for AVs to operate safely on public roads. Test-bedding AVs in complex urban environments with changing traffic volumes and a myriad of driving behaviours allow self-driving algorithms to be further developed before the next stage of validation in more complex environments in other parts of Singapore.

    A first of its kind, the Integrated Estate Management System (iEMS) connects to various Building Management Systems (BMS) to extract and analyse building equipment data such as air conditioning and mechanical ventilation systems. IEMS detects faults and provides operational recommendations to assist Facilities Management staff in resolving these issues. This also eliminates dependence on data from BMS vendors, and translates to energy savings, increased efficiency and improved user experience.

    This pilot programme saw the application of a proprietary remote sensor from the Internet of Things at Fusionopolis. With the sensor, smart devices can remotely control and manage building facilities through wi-fi, bluetooth and cloud hosting. The programme was initiated to improve customer service, increase energy savings and make remote connectivity more ef ficient while optimising the use of existing building resources.

    This newly-launched Smart Lighting System aims to provide an energy ef ficient and cost-effective wireless solution for the management of mass lightings on streets and other industrial and commercial areas. Initial testing is being conducted at the Biopolis One B2 car park using energy ef ficient LED lights and smart wireless occupancy sensors that will help the facility enhance its energy savings. For example, lights will be dimmed in the absence of pedestrians and moving vehicles. It is expected to save more than 50% of the car park's current energy consumption.

    Mediapolis

    This project seeks ideas for innovative and cost-efficient technologies that can convert excavated rocks from Jurong Rock Caverns into higher value road construction materials, such as B1 and W3B asphaltic wearing courses that meet regulatory requirements. Upon project success, the demand for granite aggregates for road construction will be reduced.

    Fusionopolis

    This project validated the benefits of BIM on mobile devices to enable Project Managers working on Fusionopolis Two to be more efficient in carrying out on-site construction management work using BIM applications running on iPads.

    This eased the workload for inspection, communication and reporting. Defects and issues were also better communicated through photos and indication of the exact locations on plan and in 3D.
