    The Chilled Ceiling is an energy-saving air distribution technology for building space cooling that uses the theory of convection and radiation to cool the room. The ceiling also uses "a phase change material" (PCM) to store cool air at night for release during the day to supplement cooling. It achieves energy savings for space cooling and is also a quieter air conditioning option compared to a conventional air conditioning system since no fan is needed for air distribution.

    The test-bed was successful with no condensation observed and with a 35% energy saving in fan power.
