    This is a cloud-based system that allows JTC to centrally and remotely monitor, analyse, and optimise estate and building systems on the facilities management front.

    The system leverages data, analytics and sensors across our buildings and estates to provide insight into how the systems are operating, thus allowing JTC to make decisions to ensure optimal operations of the buildings’ systems, and essential and critical services.     18 Bigbelly Smart Bins are deployed across 6 locations in JTC’s premises including The JTC Summit. The Smart Bins are able to gauge waste levels and activate clearing of the bins only when they are full. This optimises the collection response time and reduces frequency for waste collection, thereby reducing the manpower required to clear the bins.

    This System allows real-time monitoring of toilets’ cleanliness levels; alerting janitors when toilets should be cleaned so that they can be deployed more effectively.

    The sensors detect odour in real-time by monitoring the levels of ammonia in the toilet, triggering an alert for toilet cleaning when the odour level rises.

    Testbedding footprint: The JTC Summit

    The chilled ceiling system is an energy-saving air distribution technology that uses the theory of convection and radiation for building space cooling. The chilled water pipes are embedded onto the ceiling panels. The chilled water pipes cool the ceiling panels and provide cooling to the space by radiant heat transfer. The cold air around the ceiling is denser and therefore falls to the floor and replaces warmer air. The convection process continues. This achieves energy savings and is a quieter air conditioning option compared to a conventional system since no fans are needed for air distribution.
