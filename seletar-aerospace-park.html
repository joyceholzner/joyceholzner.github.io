    Seletar Aerospace Park (SAP) is a conducive environment for aerospace companies to operate and collaborate within. The 320-hectare development hosts a dedicated cluster of activities, including the maintenance, repair and overhaul of aircraft and components; manufacturing and assembly of aircraft engines and components; business and general aviation; as well as training and research & development. Companies in SAP reap the benefits of the integration of world-class business infrastructure and the synergy between global and local aerospace industry players.​

    Testbedding footprint: Seletar Aerospace Park

    Lightweight and flexible Organic Photovoltaic (OPV) film can be integrated on the facades of buildings, offering a larger surface area for harvesting of solar energy beyond roof tops. This test-bed seeks to verify the commercial viability of the OPV technology and benchmark it to conventional silicone-based solar cells.

    Testbedding footprint: Seletar Aerospace Park

    To address technical problems related to transplanting trees with conventional methods, a feasibility study was carried out in 2011 in Chinese Garden and JTC’s International Business Park with the development and testing of two prototype bags. The study was a success.

    The trees pre-planted in Phase 1 were subsequently transplanted to Seletar Aerospace Park in 2014 during Phase 2. Based on the success of Phase 1, Phase 3 will look to implementing the solution on a larger scale along roads slated for widening.
