Seletar Aerospace Park

The Tree Carrier project was developed to address technical problems related to transplantation of existing trees using conventional methods. Phase 1 of the Tree Carrier feasibility study was carried out in 2011, where the development and testing of two prototype bags were carried out in IBP & Chinese Gardens. The health of the trees had been monitored and was reported to be in good condition. The trees pre-planted in Phase 1 were transplanted to Seletar Aerospace Park in 2014 during Phase 2. Based on the success of the first transplantation exercise using tree carriers, a Phase 3 has been proposed to implement it in a larger scale along a road that has been chosen for widening in the future.

Mediapolis

Use of Excavated Sedimentary Rocks for Road Construction

This project seeks ideas for innovative and cost-efficient technologies that can convert excavated rocks from Jurong Rock Caverns (JRC) into higher value road construction materials, such as B1 and W3B asphaltic wearing courses that can meet the requirements of the regulatory authorities. The sedimentary rock pavement has fulfilled all the performance requirements set out in the POC stage and is currently in the POV to evaluate its performance. The success of this project will reduce the demand of granite aggregate for road construction.
