    Jurong Island is the cornerstone of Singapore's energy and chemicals industry and home to a vibrant portfolio of more than 100 leading global petroleum, petrochemical and specialty chemical companies.

    Testbedding footprint: Jurong Island

    Phytoremediation is the use of living plants to clean up soil, air and water contaminated with hazardous chemicals. This innovative study is part of the landscaping review of Jurong Island, the cornerstone of Singapore’s energy and chemicals industry. The study, conducted by NTU, NIE and SUTD, will see the selection of landscaping plants for Jurong Island for not only their aesthetic qualities, but also in consideration of their phytoremediation capabilities. Hence, these selected plants will serve dual functions of landscaping as well as phyto-remediate the pollutants that are present in their proximate environment.

    The Smart Hump System test-bed is the first test-bed in the world for a technology that utilises kinetic energy generated from moving vehicles for power generation. Based on the concept that electricity could be generated when vehicles drive over a road hump, 2-5 kilowatts of power could be generated for every 5,000 vehicles. This is enough power to light between 100 - 500 LED lamps for up to 18 hours a day. The test-bed is located at the Jurong Island Checkpoint due to the high vehicular traffic. The power generated from the system will be monitored and used to power up lights and air-conditioning at the Checkpoint’s administrative buildings.
