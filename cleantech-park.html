    Singapore's first eco-business park, CleanTech Park, is an R&D and test-bedding site for early adoption of technology and solutions. Located beside the Nanyang Technological University, the 50-hectare Park was developed with emphasis on retaining the natural environment and biodiversity of the area and serves as a choice location for forward-looking corporations that embrace environmental sustainability.

    This is a cloud-based system that allows JTC to centrally and remotely monitor, analyse, and optimise estate and building systems on the facilities management front. The system leverages data, analytics and sensors across our buildings and estates to provide insight into how the systems are operating, thus allowing JTC to make decisions to ensure optimal operations of the buildings’ essential and critical services and systems. This in turn reduces energy usage, improves the comfort of JTC’s tenants and support JTC to be more productive and efficient in managing facilities.

    18 Big Belly Smart Bins are deployed across 6 locations in JTC premises. BigBelly is able to gauge waste levels and activate clearing of the bin only when it is full. This optimises the collection response time and reduces frequency for waste collection, thereby reducing manpower requirements to clear the bins as opposed to traditional clearing of the bins even when it is not full.

    The lightweight and flexible nature of Organic Photovoltaic (OPV) film integrated with various building façades offer a larger surface area for energy generation compared to roofs. This test-bed seeks to verify the commercial viability of the OPV technology and benchmark it to conventional SI solar modules.

    Being the first agency to allow the testing of AVs on a public road network, the one-north and CleanTech Park trial sites offers opportunities to test and optimise the technical capabilities and infrastructural requirements necessary for AVs to operate safely on public roads. Test-bedding AVs in complex urban environments with changing traffic volumes and a myriad of driving behaviours allow self-driving algorithms to be further developed before the next stage of validation in more complex environments in other parts of Singapore.

    From technology invented by Omega Solar to cool the temperature of solar cells in a panel through the use of a unique cooling system, while at the same time supplying thermal heating, this test-bed seeks to supply power and hot water to the building using the new prototype while verifying if the system gives rise to improved performance of solar panels in terms of power output.

    This system envisions to recycle black water into greywater for non-potable use within a building. The test-bed enables the testing and recycling of onsite wastewater under typical building operational conditions, and allow for the optimisation of the system to take place, balancing energy requirements while meeting the needs of the building and relevant authorities.

    The HVS‐Electrochemical Activation (ECA) System treats cooling tower water non-chemically by continuously removing the scale/hardness and generating disinfectants onsite. It aims to mitigate scale, bio-fouling, corrosion and bacterial infection in Cooling Tower Water Distribution Systems. The test-bed assesses the feasibility of the HVS-ECA technology deployment for Singapore's cooling tower water in local weather conditions. It also enables establishment of optimal operation characteristics and design parameters of HVS-ECA System to document and verify water conservation, pollution prevention, and savings in operations and maintenance.

    A partnership between NTU, JTC and Induct Technologies and funded by EDB, this test-bed aims to test and optimise the driverless shuttle, NAVIA, to allow safe integration with local traffic. This paves the way for the integration of autonomous vehicles into the transport system for future application in first and last mile transportation.

    Using a unique 2-step reforming process, biomass wastes act as a sacrificial catalyst to extract high purity Hydrogen from water to drive the fuel cell. It provides DC power which is converted to 3 phase AC, and will supply part of the electrical power requirements at CleanTech Park.

    Led and executed by ERI@N, this is an A*STAR-MND project in collaboration with Memsys Clear Water Pte. Ltd. and Memsys TEC AG. The project uses membrane technology to design a liquid desiccant air-conditioning and dehumidification system which is powered by solar energy. The system increases the efficiency of the air-conditioning system and has potential energy savings of 60%.
