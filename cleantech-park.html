    Singapore's first eco-business park, CleanTech Park, is an R&D and test-bedding site for early adoption of technology and solutions. Located beside the Nanyang Technological University, the 50-hectare Park was developed with emphasis on retaining the natural environment and biodiversity of the area and serves as a choice location for forward-looking corporations that embrace environmental sustainability.

    Testbedding footprint: CleanTech Park

    Testbedding footprint: CleanTech Park

    18 Bigbelly Smart Bins are deployed across 6 locations in JTC’s premises including CleanTech Park. The Smart Bins are able to gauge waste levels and activate clearing of the bins only when they are full. This optimises the collection response time and reduces frequency for waste collection, thereby reducing the manpower required to clear the bins.

    Testbedding footprint: CleanTech Park

This test-bed seeks to verify the commercial viability of the OPV technology and benchmark it to conventional silicone-based solar cells.

    Testbedding footprint: CleanTech Park

    AV Trials

    JTC is the first government agency in Singapore to allow the testing of AVs on a public road network. The trial site in the CleanTech Park offers opportunities to test and optimise the technical capabilities and infrastructural requirements necessary for AVs to operate safely on public roads. Test-bedding AVs in complex urban environments with changing traffic volumes and a myriad of driving behaviour allow self-driving algorithms to be further developed. This is crucial in order for the AVs to be validated in more complex environments in other parts of Singapore.

    NAVYA Autonomous Vehicle

    This test-bed is a partnership between JTC, Nanyang Technological University (NTU) and Induct Technologies on an autonomous electric shuttle. The autonomous vehicle by French driverless carmaker, NAVYA, can carry eight passengers. Currently plying the test route between CleanTech Park and NTU, this could pave the way for the integration of autonomous vehicles in Singapore’s transport system to address the “first mile, last mile” transport problem faced by cities.

    Testbedding footprint: CleanTech Park

    Led and executed by ERI@N, this is an A*STAR-MND project in collaboration with Memsys Clear Water Pte. Ltd. and Memsys TEC AG.     This is an alert system to feedback any anomalies detected during street lighting operations (i.e. main power failure), to shorten the turnaround time for repair works.
